How do Oscar winners stack up against top-grossing movies at the box office?


By Sohani Goonetillake

Box office heavyweights ‘Barbie’ and ‘Oppenheimer’ racked up 21 Oscar nominations this year. 'Oppenheimer' walked away with seven Oscars including Best Picture. If you’re struggling to remember the last time a blockbuster hit took home Best Picture, it’s because it hasn't happened for a while...

We examined the box office success of Best Picture winners over the last thirty years to see if commercial success influences critical acclaim. Here's what we found:

The revenue of top-grossing movies have increased over the last few decades.

Even after a dramatic drop in 2020 because of the pandemic, 'Spiderman: No Way Home' became the second-highest grossing film.

Comparatively, the domestic gross of Best Picture winners have declined over the years.

'Titanic', 'Forrest Gump' and 'Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King' are the only Best Picture winners that have also ruled the box office in recent years.

On average, over the last 10 years, top-grossing movies racked up a whopping 13 times more revenue than Best Picture winners at the Oscars between 2014 and 2023.

The huge disparity between what films the critics recognise and what moviegoers enjoy is a recent trend. If we go back even further than what's pictured, we'd find that to be true. Think of popular films like “Rain Man,” “Braveheart,” “Rocky,” “The Godfather,” and “The Sound of Music” to name a few. So what movies are behind this trend? Let's take a closer look at the disparity in the last 10 years and see which movies were regarded by critics and movie-goers.


The movies that were selected by critics are almost exclusively Dramas. While moviegoers opt for Action and Adventure above all else.

Just take a look at this analysis of the top genres. Action and Adventure rein supreme and when you think of two of the three titles that won Best Picture and ruled the box office, Titanic and LOTR are both action movies.
